Safety Tips for Using Artificial Intelligence

AI can be helpful, but students must use it carefully, responsibly, and safely

Essential Safety Tips

Check the Information

AI can make mistakes, so always fact-check important answers using trusted websites, school databases, textbooks, or academic journals. Compare AI responses with information from multiple reliable sources before accepting them as true.

Do Not Share Personal Information

Never type passwords, addresses, phone numbers, school names, student ID numbers, or other private details into AI tools. Treat AI like you would treat any stranger online. Keep your personal information safe and secure.

Use AI as a Helper, Not a Replacement

AI should support your learning, not do all your thinking and schoolwork for you. Use AI to understand concepts better, get ideas, or check your work, but do your own research, thinking, and writing. Your own learning is what matters most.

Watch Out for Bias and Unfair Answers

AI can have bias or be unfair because of the data it was trained on. Always think critically about AI responses. Ask yourself: Does this seem fair? Are all perspectives included? Are there other ways to look at this? Use your own judgment.

Ask a Teacher or Trusted Adult When Unsure

If something seems confusing, unsafe, or incorrect, don't hesitate to ask your teacher, parent, librarian, or another trusted adult. They can help you understand AI better and make safe decisions online.

Good communication with trusted adults is an important part of staying safe online. Never feel embarrassed to ask questions or report concerns.

How Students Should Use AI Responsibly

Using AI responsibly means making ethical choices and being aware of how your actions affect others. Here's how students can use AI tools the right way:

  • Be ethical: Use AI for learning and improvement, not to cheat or plagiarize others' work. Always give credit to sources and cite AI use in your assignments as instructed.

  • Think critically: Question AI responses. Research multiple sources. Develop your own opinions and conclusions, not just accept what AI tells you.

  • Make safe choices: Be respectful online. Do not use AI to create harmful, mean, or inappropriate content. Remember that your actions online can affect real people.

  • Balance technology and real life: Use AI as one tool among many, but don't spend all your time on screens. Real-world activities, friendships, and face-to-face learning are also important.

Remember These Three Rules

1

Think Before You Type

Consider what you are asking AI. Is the question safe? Will the response help you learn?

2

Check Before You Trust

Verify AI answers using trusted sources. Don't assume everything AI says is correct.

3

Learn, Do Not Copy

Use AI to understand concepts better, but always do your own work and thinking.
